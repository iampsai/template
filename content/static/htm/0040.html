Recognize Signs of Threats

LESSON 4 of 9

We'll talk about how to spot potential threats to your devices and what you can do to protect them. Catching signs of malicious activity early can help prevent security breaches and keep your personal and work-related data safe. Here are some essential things to watch out for:

  • Be vigilant for unusual activity on your device

    Pay attention to any strange behavior on your device, like unexpected pop-ups, slow performance, or unfamiliar applications. These could be signs that your device has been compromised or infected with malware.

  • Watch out for phishing emails or messages

    Be on the lookout for phishing emails or messages that trick you into giving away sensitive information or downloading harmful software. These scams can put your personal and work data at risk. Be cautious when opening emails from unknown senders or clicking on suspicious links. Recognizing and avoiding phishing attempts is critical to protecting your devices and information from cyber threats.

Secure Communication

LESSON 5 of 9

We'll cover how to safely share and protect sensitive information from unauthorized access and cyber threats. Good communication practices are essential for keeping your data confidential and secure. Here are some key strategies to follow:

  • Use secure communication tools provided by your organization 

    When sharing sensitive information, always use your organization's secure tools, such as encrypted email or messaging apps. These tools protect your data from being intercepted or accessed by unauthorized parties.

  • Avoid using public or unsecured Wi-Fi networks 

    Avoid public or unsecured Wi-Fi networks, especially when dealing with work-related tasks. These networks are vulnerable to eavesdropping and data interception by hackers. Instead, encrypt your connection using secure and trusted networks with encryption or a virtual private network (VPN).
