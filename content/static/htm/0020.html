What is BYOD

LESSON 1 of 9

BYOD stands for "Bring Your Own Device" is a policy where employees use their personal devices like smartphones or laptops for work tasks instead of relying solely on company-provided devices.

Understand the Risks

LESSON 2 of 9

We'll look at the main risks of using personal devices for work and how to handle them. While using your own devices for work is convenient, it can also make you vulnerable to security issues that need to be managed to protect both your personal and company data. Here are three key risks to keep in mind:

Personal devices sometimes have weaker security features than company-provided ones, which puts them at greater risk of malware and cyber-attacks. Therefore, it is essential to take extra steps to protect individual devices when using them for work.

Mixing personal and work-related data on one device poses a significant risk. It makes it easier for data breaches and unauthorized access to happen. Having both types of data together increases the chance of mistakes or someone taking advantage of the situation.

Losing or having a personal device stolen when it's used for work can be harmful. It could mean that confidential company information gets into the wrong hands. That's why it's crucial to have strong security measures.
